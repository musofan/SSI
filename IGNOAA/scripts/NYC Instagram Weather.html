NOAA handbook¶

http://www.nws.noaa.gov/om/forms/resources/SFCTraining.pdf

According to the NOAA training handbook for weather observers, sky cover is determined as follows (briefly):

The cloud cover is split into layers, with a max of three. It is then determined how much of the sky is covered in the first layer. The measurement of cloud cover at the second and third layers is a cumulative. That's good for us, because all we want to know is how much of the sky is covered. Layers are given heights in hundreds of feet, but we won't bother with that information, since it's not clear how we'd use it to measure anything affecting people on the ground.

The Sky Variable¶

Instagram datetimes¶

Then, we need to adjust the IG datetimes because they actually don't measure local time! This is an important lesson about data science - something as simple as a timezone mismatch could really throw off your analysis!

the join¶

Okay. Now that we have both our weather and IG records with datetimes, we need to join on those datetimes. But we can't do a join yet, because the two datetimes don't match very well: the IG datetimes are organically-generated and all over the place. The weather datetimes are at regular observation intervals. What we want to know, for any given IG datetime, is the closest weather datetime. In order to find that, we will iterate through all the IG datetimes, subtracting every weather datetime (within a small range) from each and finding the minimum difference. Whatever weather datetime we subtracted to produce the minimum, that will be assigned the IG datetime.

In [75]:
# the major operation

resolution = []

for item in instagram.updated:
    
    # make tmp series with only those timestamps within a certain range
    
    margin = datetime.timedelta(hours=1)
    tmp = weather.resolution[[item - margin <= member <= item + margin for member in weather.resolution]]
    
    # crucial step: find the closest weather datetime; the 'key' argument for min() makes this pretty easy
    if len(tmp) == 0:
        resolution.append('nan')
    else: 
        a = min(tmp, key = lambda x: abs(item - x))
        resolution.append(a)

instagram['resolution'] = resolution
